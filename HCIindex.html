Initial Pitch

User needs

In crowded places, people can have difficulty locating a person of interest. For example, looking for a specific person at a conference, a friend at a festival, a guide at a museum, your child at an amusement park, so someone you’re looking to interact with in person. Since this is a problem where the two users would be in close proximity, GPS and other mapping applications are not precise enough.

Limitations

In the settings described above, the user is basically brute-forcing their way through the search for their person of interest. There is no way to find the person of interest other than looking and asking around or calling them, but this not always possible (if you don’t have the phone number, or the person’s cell phone is out of power, etc.). This can result in a lot of wasted time.

Interaction

The person of interest would be given the wearable device at the entrance point of the event or location. They would then simply have to have it on them and turned on, in a pocket or in their bag. The user looking for that person would use a mobile device to see an orientation and distance to the person of interest and simply have to travel in that direction to meet them, using visible and/or non-visible cues.
